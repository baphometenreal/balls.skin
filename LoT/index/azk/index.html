Azkali State (1967-2023)

Azkali State (Azkali: Azkali Herria; Inori: Aścali Heriścae; Hakainaki: Afukali Etorifun) was a country in southern Ukilen, with an area of approximately [placeholder] square miles. The Azkali State shared borders with Sunteria and Baphia to the west and north respectively, as well as maritime borders with Loremesia and Hallei to the west and southwest. At its peak, the Azkali State had a population of over 42 million, concentrated mostly in the west of the country. Its capital was the city of Chekearoa, and its largest and most influential city was Otzeipao.

The Azkali State was first declared on the 19th of August 1967, following the unification of the three largest Azkali nations at the time: the Grand Duchy of Otzeipao-Chekearoken, the Independent State of Azkali, and the Azkali Republic in late 1966. The Azkali State was unstable, and faced several revolts and separatist movements throughout its existence.

History

Foundation of the Azkali State

The Azkali State was formed from the Azkali Provisional Government (1966-1967), which controlled the areas held by the Grand Duchy of Otzeipao-Chekearoken, the Independent State of Azkali, and the Azkali Republic following their unifications in November of 1966. Elections were held on the 20th of August 1966, and the first president, Ari Kaiskahouta, running under the Social Democratic Party of Azkali, was sworn in on December 31st of that year.
